## Finding the Right Translation

Choosing a Quran translation is an important decision. The best translation balances accuracy with readability in your language. Here is a guide to the most respected translations available on Kuran.studio.

## English Translations

### Sahih International
The most widely used modern English translation. Known for its clarity and accuracy. Used by major Islamic websites and apps worldwide.

### Hilali-Khan
A more literal translation with extensive footnotes. Popular in academic settings.

## European Languages

### French — Muhammad Hamidullah
The gold standard of French Quran translations. Hamidullah's work is praised for its literary quality and scholarly accuracy.

### German — Frank Bubenheim
The most respected German translation, produced with collaboration from the Saudi Ministry of Islamic Affairs.

### Spanish — Isa Garcia
A modern translation that reads naturally in Spanish while maintaining fidelity to the Arabic text.

### Italian — Rowwad Translation Center
A contemporary Italian translation with clear, accessible language.

## Balkan Languages

### Albanian — Hasan Efendi Nahi
A beloved Albanian translation known for its beautiful prose and scholarly footnotes. The primary translation used by Albanian-speaking Muslims.

### Bosnian — Besim Korkut
The most widely-read Bosnian translation, known for its elegance and clarity.

### Turkish — Rowwad Translation Center
A modern Turkish translation with contemporary language that is easy to understand.

## Asian Languages

### Urdu — Muhammad Junagarhi
The most popular Urdu translation, widely used across Pakistan and India.

### Indonesian — Muhammad Quraish Shihab
A scholarly Indonesian translation with detailed commentary.

### Chinese — Ma Jian (Ma Suliman)
The most widely-used Chinese Quran translation, accessible to Mandarin speakers worldwide.

## How to Choose

When selecting a translation:

1. **Pick your native language** — Reading in your mother tongue provides the deepest understanding
2. **Consider footnotes** — Some translations include scholarly notes that explain context
3. **Try multiple versions** — Many languages have several translations available on Kuran.studio
4. **Compare with Arabic** — Kuran.studio shows Arabic text alongside every translation

All translations on Kuran.studio are sourced from [QuranEnc.com](https://quranenc.com), a trusted platform maintained by scholars.
